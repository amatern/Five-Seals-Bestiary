# Five Seals — World Lore

*This file is the source of truth for the game's setting. Claude Code reads this before generating any narrative content — creature names, flavor text, move names, NPC trainers, region themes. When in doubt, defer to this document or ask.*

*Items marked `[SPOILER]` are reserved for tabletop reveals. Do not surface them in the game, even indirectly. They are listed here so generated content does not accidentally hint at them.*

---

## The world

The campaign is set on the **northern Sword Coast** of the Forgotten Realms, post–Spell Plague, current era. The party is based out of **Phandalin**, a small frontier town being rebuilt. The threat is regional in scope at the start and existential by the end.

The driving truth of the world is this: **the dragon Infernadax sleeps beneath Crimson Peak, sealed by five enchantments three hundred years ago. The seals are failing.** The Stone seal has already broken. The others are weakening.

The mood is grim but not without hope. Heroes have done this before — they just paid for it.

---

## The Five Seals

Five enchantments created three hundred years ago by seven heroes led by **Luminara the Radiant**. Each seal binds a portion of Infernadax's power. Each was powered by sacrifice — Luminara's soul, the Halfling Healer's soul shattered into five fragments, and the lives of four others now forgotten by history.

The seals are not abstract — each is a place, a gemstone, and a guardian.

| Seal | Location | Gem | Guardian | Status |
|------|----------|-----|----------|--------|
| Stone | Crimson Peak | Crimson Heart Ruby | None (long dead) | **Broken** |
| Water | The Weeping Depths | Sapphire of the Depths | Triton/water keeper (gone silent) | Weakening (~80% corrupted) |
| Wind | Stormcrest Spire | Topaz of the Storm | Storm Giant clan (3 of 8 dead) | Intact, under siege |
| Light | The Radiant Temple | Diamond of Dawn | Order of the Radiant Dawn (~5 elderly clerics) | Weakening (~60% corrupted, eternal flame dimming) |
| Shadow | The Umbral Vault | Emerald of Twilight | Shadow monk order | Intact — but compromised from within `[SPOILER: Nyx has infiltrated; Infernadax's phylactery is hidden here]` |

Each broken seal makes Infernadax stronger and weakens the barrier between planes.

## Infernadax the Eternal Flame

**Ancient Red Dragon. 800+ years old. Sealed beneath Crimson Peak. Awakening.**

Infernadax is the central antagonist. He is patient, brilliant, theatrical, and ancient. He speaks. He taunts. He remembers.

His true purpose `[SPOILER]`: he is **Tiamat's Herald on the Sword Coast**. His freedom tears the veil between planes and allows Tiamat herself to manifest. The Five Seals are not just holding back a dragon — they are holding back the Queen of Evil Dragons.

His other secret `[SPOILER]`: three hundred years ago he created a **phylactery** and hid it in the Umbral Vault. If he is killed without the phylactery being destroyed first, he rises as a **dracolich**. Only the necromancer Unpickled can sense it.

### Voice and presence

Infernadax does not appear directly until late in the campaign, but his voice is felt throughout — in dreams, through flames, projected through servants. When he speaks:

- Deep, resonant, deliberate. He savors every word.
- Archaic phrasing — "thou," "mayhaps," "little ones," "mayflies," "children of ash."
- Speaks in absolutes ("You **will** fail"), not hedges.
- Refers to himself in the third person when being grandiose.
- Patient. Never rushed. Always in control.
- Personal — he knows the heroes' backstories and exploits them.

He should be present in the game as a *voice* (loading screens, battle intros, lore-tome flavor text), not as a creature you can battle. Encountering Infernadax himself is the final act of the campaign, not a sandbox encounter.

## The Seal Breakers — chromatic dragon servants

These are Infernadax's lieutenants. Each one is sent to break a seal. **These are the prestige creatures of the game** — they should exist in the bestiary from the start as canon entries.

### Thessalmar the Drowned — Adult Blue Dragon
Patient, methodical, cruel. Corrupting the Seal of Water by drowning its guardian and poisoning the depths. Lightning breath works underwater. Lair: flooded caverns beneath the Weeping Depths. Servants: sahuagin and water cultists.

### Silvaclaw the Storm Eater — Young White Dragon
Savage but cunning. Hunting the Storm Giants one by one to break the Seal of Wind. Lair: ice caves on Stormcrest Spire. Servants: ice trolls, winter wolves, yetis. The youngest and least experienced of the four — closer to feral than calculating.

### Vexmire the Dawnsbane — Adult Black Dragon
Sadistic. Patient. Remembers. **Was alive during the original sealing 300 years ago** and recognizes Fiona as Luminara reborn — carries a personal vendetta. Poisoning the eternal flame at the Radiant Temple, corrupting the swamp around it. Servants: dragon cultists, shadows, undead.

### Nyx the Shadow Fang — Adult Green Dragon
Master manipulator. Has **infiltrated** the shadow monk order at the Umbral Vault, turning monks against each other. Guards the phylactery `[SPOILER]` for Infernadax. Servants: corrupted monks, doppelgangers.

### Xarrath the Unseen — Red Dragonborn Assassin (not a dragon, but a key servant)
Predator-coded — invisibility, heat-vision, glowing blood, plasma glaive. Honorable in his way — won't kill helpless prey. Hunts the party throughout the campaign. May eventually turn against Infernadax as a "tyrant unworthy of his service." Could appear as a recurring opponent and later as an unlockable ally.

## Tiamat — the looming shadow

`[SPOILER]` Tiamat, the five-headed Queen of Evil Dragons, is the deeper threat behind Infernadax. As seals break, her influence on the world grows:

- 1 seal broken (current state): chromatic dragons emboldened, cult recruiting surges, minor planar bleed
- 2 seals: dragons attack settlements openly, Tiamat sends visions, chromatic dragons gain stat bonuses
- 3 seals: the sky turns red at dusk, Draconic becomes a magical language, wyrmlings spontaneously appear
- 4 seals: reality tears, glimpses of the Nine Hells, dragons gain legendary resistance, Tiamat can possess followers
- 5 seals: a portal opens. Tiamat manifests one hour per day, then permanently if not stopped.

In the game, Tiamat is referenced obliquely — "the Dragon Queen," "the Five-Headed," "She Who Waits Beyond the Veil." She is never directly named in player-facing text unless the player has already reached that part of the campaign.

## The Original Seven Heroes (300 years ago)

The heroes who first sealed Infernadax. Three are remembered. Four have been forgotten.

- **Luminara the Radiant** — Aasimar paladin/cleric. Led the sealing. Sacrificed her life to power the seals. `[SPOILER: reincarnated as Fiona in the current party]`
- **Torax the Defiant** — Red Dragonborn fighter, Crimson Guard. **Betrayed the others** — tried to steal the dragon's power. Was cursed for it; his bloodline burns from within forever. `[SPOILER: ancestor of Chad]`
- **The Halfling Healer** — A cleric whose name is lost. Split his soul into five pieces, one per seal, to bind the magic. `[SPOILER: reincarnated as Dave]`
- **The Other Four** — Names forgotten by time. Each contributed power to one seal. Likely died in the sealing.

Of the four lost heroes, one of them may have been an elf seer, a halfling who stole something from the dragon's hoard, a tabaxi clan-warrior, or a human necromancer who understood how to bind death itself. The campaign has not confirmed which. The game may treat them as legendary figures with names like **The Forgotten Four**, **The Nameless Seers**, **The Vanished**.

## The Current Heroes — Chad's party

The seven player characters in the current campaign. **These are not creatures in the game's bestiary** — they are the heroes whose journey the game is set against. They may appear as **trainer NPCs** in later content, never as designable creatures.

- **Chad** — Red Dragonborn Barbarian. Last descendant of Torax. Carries the burning curse. *Jonas's character.*
- **Fiona** — Aasimar Barbarian. Reincarnation of Luminara.
- **Dave** — Halfling Cleric. Reincarnation of the Halfling Healer; his soul is fragmented across the five seal-gems.
- **Arlo** — Elf Cleric. Starbound Prophet. Has seen that five of seven will fall.
- **Sprinkles** — Halfling Rogue. Cursed bloodline; must possess the Crimson Heart Ruby.
- **Unpickled** — Human Wizard, Necromancer. Only one who can sense the phylactery.
- **Vendella** — Tabaxi Rogue. Last of the Dragon Claw Clan; her birthmark reveals dragon weak points.

Chad is special: **he is the player's son's character**. Treat him with appropriate reverence in any flavor text or content that references him.

## The Prophecy

Spoken by Arlo to himself, in dreams. Known in part to the party. The full text is reserved.

What the party knows:
> *Seven shall stand before the Eternal Flame.*
> *Five shall fall.*
> *Two shall remain.*
> *The scales will be balanced in blood and sacrifice.*

`[SPOILER: the full prophecy]`:
> *Seven shall stand, bound by threads of ancient blood*
> *Five shall fall to claw and flame and shadow*
> *Two shall remain when dawn breaks over ash*
> *The Chronicler will write of sacrifice*
> *The Prophet will choose who stands at the end*
> *The scales will be balanced in blood and starlight*

The Chronicler is Dave. The Prophet is Arlo. The prophecy may be true but is not fixed — the Loom of Fate in the Feywild can alter it.

In-game, the Prophecy can be referenced obliquely in flavor text. Phrases like *"the scales will be balanced," "five shall fall," "dawn breaks over ash"* fit the tone perfectly. Do not surface the full text or the spoiler identities.

## Key locations

- **Phandalin** — frontier town. Heroes' home base. Rebuilt after recent troubles. Sister Garaele runs the temple of Tymora and knows the lore of the seals.
- **Crimson Peak** — volcanic mountain, broken Seal of Stone, Infernadax's prison. The land around it is scorched and unstable.
- **The Weeping Depths** — underwater cave system. Cold, vast, lightless. Water glows red at night now.
- **Stormcrest Spire** — the highest mountain in the region. Storm giants live near the summit; eternal blizzards.
- **The Radiant Temple** — once a beacon of light, now sinking into a corrupted swamp. The eternal flame is dimming.
- **The Umbral Vault** — a shadow monastery carved into a black cliff. Quiet, ascetic, deceptive.
- **The Whispering Woods** — where the Dragon Claw Clan died 50 years ago hunting Infernadax. Their camp and research remain.
- **The Feywild (Loom of Fate)** — accessed through a portal. Where prophecy can be unmade. `[SPOILER: optional Session 14 destination]`

## Factions

- **The Cult of the Dragon (Reborn)** — humanoid cultists following Tiamat through Infernadax. Led by Wyrmspeakers (one per chromatic color), elite Dragon Fangs, and rank-and-file Dragonclaws. They are working to break all five seals.
- **The Crimson Guard** — order of dragonborn warriors sworn to fight dragons. Effectively extinct; **Ember Scaleheart** is the last.
- **The Order of the Radiant Dawn** — the elderly clerics defending the Seal of Light. Fewer than a dozen remain.
- **The Shadow Monks of the Umbral Vault** — ascetics guarding the Seal of Shadow. Compromised by Nyx's infiltration.
- **The Storm Giant clans of Stormcrest** — eight giants reduced to five. Honorable, ancient, willing to ally.
- **The Dragon Claw Clan** — tabaxi dragon-hunters, wiped out 50 years ago. Vendella is the last survivor.

## Naming conventions

When generating creature names, move names, or location names, default to:

- **Two-word compounds:** *Bone Singer, Ash Wyrm, Hollow Saint, Cinder Lord, Storm Eater, Shadow Fang, Eternal Flame, Crimson Heart*
- **Single archaic words:** *Wyrm, Drake, Saint, Crone, Herald, Sentinel, Effigy, Reliquary*
- **Possessive titles:** *The Dawnsbane, The Drowned, The Unseen, The Eternal Flame*
- **Elemental + concept:** *Ashen Choir, Drowned Reliquary, Twilight Vault, Stormcrest, Crimson Peak*

Avoid: cute/punny names, anglicized modern compounds (e.g. "Firewing," "Shadowstep"), Pokémon-style mashups, anything that ends in "-mon" or "-zard."

Vocabulary that fits: *smolder, riven, gnawed, hollowed, unburied, ashen, drowned, crimson, sundered, unmade, eternal, herald, reliquary, sanctum, vault, choir, sentinel.*

## Game-specific creative direction

When the game generates flavor text for a creature, the voice should sound like a passage from a dusty bestiary kept in a tower — terse, ominous, lightly archaic. One or two sentences. Examples (game-original, not from the campaign):

> *A wyrm born in the ashes of the Second Seal's breaking. Its scales smolder even after death.*

> *Cousin to the great shadow-dragons of the Umbral Vault, though smaller and far more spiteful. It feeds on grief.*

> *The Crimson Guard once trained these to track dragons by scent of brimstone. None remain who can command them.*

> *Found only in the ruins of Phandalin's outer wards. It speaks in the voices of the recently drowned.*

When generating move names, default to ominous and short:

> *Ember Breath, Tail Lash, Ash Shroud, Infernal Roar, Drowning Tide, Sundering Strike, Hollow Gaze, Bone Hymn, Reliquary's Curse, Twilight Veil*
