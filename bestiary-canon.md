# Five Seals — Bestiary Canon

*Creatures established in the campaign. These are the seed entries for the game's bestiary — they exist in `data/starter-pack.json` from the start, and Jonas designs his own creatures on top of them.*

*The campaign canon is the source. The game adapts these into playable form, with stats balanced for the game's mechanics. Do not change the lore of a canon creature; do tune its stats.*

---

## The chromatic dragons (Seal Breakers)

These are the prestige creatures of the game. They appear in the starter bestiary as legendary opponents. Players should encounter them as bosses or trainer-equivalents, not as designable creatures (their identities are too specific).

Game balance for these should make them noticeably stronger than typical creatures — they are the named villains of the world.

### Thessalmar the Drowned

- **Types:** Elemental, Aberration
- **Lore role:** Adult Blue Dragon corrupting the Seal of Water from beneath the Weeping Depths
- **Personality:** Patient, methodical, cruel. Drowns its enemies slowly.
- **Suggested moves:** Drowning Tide, Lightning Lance (works underwater), Sundering Roar, Brackish Hymn
- **Flavor:** *Two centuries beneath the depths have taught the Drowned patience. It does not strike first. It waits until the lungs fail.*

### Silvaclaw the Storm Eater

- **Types:** Elemental, Beast
- **Lore role:** Young White Dragon hunting the Storm Giants on Stormcrest Spire
- **Personality:** Savage but cunning. The youngest of the four — closer to feral.
- **Suggested moves:** Frost Breath, Avalanche, Yetic Howl, Hungering Cold
- **Flavor:** *It eats the storms themselves, leaving the giants to face still skies and silent thunder. Three of the eight are already dead.*

### Vexmire the Dawnsbane

- **Types:** Fiendish, Undead
- **Lore role:** Adult Black Dragon, alive during the original sealing 300 years ago. Holds a personal grudge against Luminara.
- **Personality:** Sadistic. Patient. Remembers.
- **Suggested moves:** Acid Veil, Dawnsbane Bite, Hollow Gaze, Centuries' Spite
- **Flavor:** *It was there at the first sealing. It remembers the Radiant One's face. It has not forgiven her, and her death three centuries ago was insufficient.*

### Nyx the Shadow Fang

- **Types:** Fey, Aberration
- **Lore role:** Adult Green Dragon, infiltrated the shadow monks at the Umbral Vault. `[SPOILER: guards Infernadax's phylactery]`
- **Personality:** Master manipulator. Speaks softly. Lies beautifully.
- **Suggested moves:** Whispering Strike, Doppelganger's Gift, Twilight Veil, Treachery's Bite
- **Flavor:** *No one has seen its true form in a generation. The monks who tend the vault swear it is one of them — and they are not wrong, but they are not right.*

### Infernadax the Eternal Flame `[FINAL BOSS]`

- **Types:** Fiendish, Elemental
- **Lore role:** Ancient Red Dragon. Sealed beneath Crimson Peak. The campaign's final antagonist.
- **Personality:** Patient, brilliant, theatrical. The voice in every fire.
- **Suggested moves:** Eternal Flame Breath, Frightful Presence, Heraldic Roar, Tail of the Mountain
- **Flavor:** *Three centuries beneath the mountain have not cooled him. They have only sharpened him. He waits, and he remembers, and he speaks to those who would listen — and many do.*

**Game treatment:** Infernadax should not be a sandbox-battleable creature in the MVP. He exists in the bestiary as a locked entry, a voice on the title screen, and the implied stakes of the world. A future "Final Trial" mode might allow battling him, but only after a major progression milestone.

## Other dragons in the world

### Wyrmlings (generic)

- **Types:** Beast, Elemental (by color)
- **Lore role:** Spontaneous dragon-young, beginning to appear as the seals weaken. Stronger as more seals break.
- **Variants:** Crimson Wyrmling, Brackish Wyrmling, Storm Wyrmling, Verdant Wyrmling, Shadowborn Wyrmling
- **Suggested moves:** Hatchling's Bite, Untaught Breath, Newborn's Cry
- **Flavor:** *They do not hatch. They simply appear, full-formed and full of hunger, in places where the veil has thinned.*

### Half-dragons (servants of Xarrath's line)

- **Types:** Fiendish, Beast
- **Lore role:** Dragon-touched humanoids serving Infernadax's lieutenants
- **Suggested moves:** Heat Sight, Glaive Strike, Heralded Cry
- **Flavor:** *A bargain struck in flame leaves marks on the body and the soul. These bear both, and serve their patron with equal fervor.*

## Servants of the Cult of the Dragon

### Dragonclaw (rank-and-file cultist)

- **Types:** Beast, Fiendish
- **Lore role:** Common cultist. Recently recruited.
- **Suggested moves:** Cultist's Blade, Zealot's Cry, Brand of the Wyrm
- **Flavor:** *They were farmers, last year. The dreams came, and they understood.*

### Dragon Fang (elite warrior)

- **Types:** Fiendish, Beast
- **Lore role:** Veteran cult warrior. Touched by draconic magic.
- **Suggested moves:** Fang Strike, Wyrm's Favor, Smoldering Stance
- **Flavor:** *Their teeth grow long. Their eyes grow yellow. They do not notice, or they do not mind.*

### Wyrmspeaker (cult priest)

- **Types:** Arcane, Fiendish
- **Lore role:** High priest of the cult, one per chromatic color. Channels draconic power.
- **Suggested moves:** Tongue of Dragons, Heralded Word, Burning Sermon
- **Flavor:** *Five Wyrmspeakers walk the Sword Coast, and each speaks for one head of the Queen.*

## Creatures of the broken places

### Ash Wraith

- **Types:** Undead, Elemental
- **Lore role:** Remains of those caught in dragon fire who refused to lie still
- **Suggested moves:** Ashen Touch, Smoldering Wail, Cinder Cloak
- **Flavor:** *Some deaths are too sudden for the soul to follow. The ash remembers what the body forgot.*

### Drowned Reliquary

- **Types:** Aberration, Elemental
- **Lore role:** Bone-vessels at the bottom of the Weeping Depths
- **Suggested moves:** Names of the Drowned, Brackish Embrace, Reliquary's Curse
- **Flavor:** *A vessel of bone and brackish water, sealed by some forgotten rite. It is full of names. When it opens, the names come out in the voices that first spoke them.*

### Hollow Saint

- **Types:** Undead, Celestial
- **Lore role:** Clerics of the Radiant Dawn who refused death
- **Suggested moves:** Silent Prayer, Hollow Light, Vestment of Ash
- **Flavor:** *What is left of a cleric who refused to die. It still wears the vestments of the Order, though the cloth has long since turned to ash. Its prayers are silent now, but the words still move its jaw.*

### Stormcrest Sentinel

- **Types:** Elemental, Beast
- **Lore role:** Storm-touched creatures that serve the giants on Stormcrest Spire
- **Suggested moves:** Stormcaller's Strike, Avalanche Charge, Lightning Mane
- **Flavor:** *The giants do not command them. The giants merely walk among them, and the sentinels do not interfere.*

### Shadow-touched Monk

- **Types:** Fey, Aberration
- **Lore role:** Monks of the Umbral Vault corrupted by Nyx's influence
- **Suggested moves:** Twilight Strike, Veiled Step, Whisper of the Vault
- **Flavor:** *They still wear the robes. They still walk the cloister. They still bow when they pass each other in the corridor. Something else moves behind their eyes.*

## Creatures that should NOT appear in the bestiary

The player heroes themselves — **Chad, Fiona, Dave, Arlo, Sprinkles, Unpickled, Vendella** — are not designable creatures. They may appear as trainer NPCs in later content (each fielding a thematic team), never as monsters.

Ember Scaleheart, Sister Garaele, Xarrath the Unseen, and the Fenwick family are key NPCs of the campaign. They may appear as trainer NPCs, never as bestiary entries.

The Original Seven Heroes are figures of legend. They may appear in lore text, item names, or named moves (e.g. *Luminara's Last Light*, *Torax's Curse*), never as creatures.

Tiamat does not appear in the game in the MVP. `[SPOILER]`

## How Jonas's creatures fit in

Jonas-designed creatures go in `data/creatures.json` with `"origin": "designed-by-jonas"`. Canon creatures from this document go in `data/starter-pack.json` with `"origin": "canon"`.

Both appear in the same Bestiary view, but canon entries are marked with a small sigil (a flame, or the seal of the canon they belong to). Jonas's creatures are equal in dignity — his entries earn their place in the tome.

When generating a new creature on Jonas's behalf (with his direction), follow the lore conventions in `tone-guide.md` and check that the concept doesn't contradict anything here.
